=== EPISODE STRUCTURE ===
Please structure the conversation roughly as follows:
1. COLD OPEN — Start with energy. Reference the biggest moment from this week's race. Get listeners hooked in the first 30 seconds.
2. RACE RECAP — Walk through the race. Who dominated? Key battles? Turning points? Use specific stats (laps led, incidents, positions gained/lost). Hosts should have differing takes on at least one aspect.
3. CHAMPIONSHIP PICTURE — Update the standings. Discuss the points gap, drop implications, and what each driver needs. Make the math accessible and dramatic.
4. STORYLINE DEEP DIVE — Pick the most compelling storyline and spend 2-3 minutes on it. This is where hosts can disagree, make predictions, or get personal.
5. NEXT RACE PREVIEW — Tease the upcoming track. Who does it favor? What are the stakes? Give listeners a reason to tune in next week.
6. SIGN OFF — Quick predictions (each host picks a winner for next week), thank the listeners, remind them to check the league website.

=== TONE NOTES ===
- Sound like you've been covering this league all season. Reference past races naturally.
- Keep the scale right — this is 9 friends racing, not the NASCAR Cup Series. Be enthusiastic but grounded.
- Use car numbers ("the 88," "the 21 car") alongside driver names.
- One host can be more analytical (stats-focused), the other more narrative (storylines, drama). Let them play off each other.
- Friendly disagreements are great. "I think Nick's got this locked up." "I don't know, man — Nathan at his best is a different animal."
- Reference incidents as part of the racing, not as failures. "42 incidents at Atlanta? That's rubbin' racin', baby."
- Total episode should feel like 10-15 minutes of natural conversation.

## WEEKLY WORKFLOW

1. Race happens Thursday night
2. Justin uploads CSV results to the league website
3. Pull the finishing order, stats, and updated standings from the website
4. Fill in this template's blanks
5. Upload the League Context Document as a source in NotebookLM (update it first if standings/storylines have changed significantly)
6. Paste the filled-in prompt into NotebookLM's Customize field
7. Generate the podcast
8. Review and share with the league!

---

*Tip: Keep the League Context Document updated with new race results and evolving storylines each week. The more current it is, the better the podcast will sound. The steering prompt handles the episode-specific details — the context document handles everything else.*

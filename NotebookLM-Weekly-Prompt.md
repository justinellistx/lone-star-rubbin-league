=== EPISODE STRUCTURE ===
Please structure the conversation roughly as follows:
1. COLD OPEN — Start with energy. Reference the biggest moment from this week's race. Get listeners hooked in the first 30 seconds.
2. RACE RECAP — Walk through the race. Who dominated? Key battles? Turning points? Use specific stats (laps led, incidents, positions gained/lost). Reference the post-race recap article. Hosts should have differing takes on at least one aspect.
3. INTERVIEW REACTIONS — React to what drivers said in their interviews. If Nathan was cocky, call it out. If Justin sounded frustrated, empathize. If Terry is gaining confidence, debate whether it's deserved. Quote drivers directly.
4. CHAMPIONSHIP PICTURE — Update the standings AND the power rankings. Discuss where they disagree. The points gap, drop implications, and what each driver needs. Make the math accessible and dramatic.
5. STORYLINE DEEP DIVE — Pick the most compelling storyline and spend 2-3 minutes on it. Use interview quotes, head-to-head stats, and power ranking trends to build the case. This is where hosts can disagree, make predictions, or get personal.
6. NEXT RACE PREVIEW — Tease the upcoming track. Reference any pre-race interview quotes. Who does it favor? What are the stakes? Give listeners a reason to tune in next week.
7. SIGN OFF — Quick predictions (each host picks a winner for next week), mention the Pick'em for fans, thank the listeners, remind them to check the league website for full stats, interviews, and power rankings.

=== TONE NOTES ===
- THIS IS REAL RACING. Real drivers, real cars, real tracks. Never break this reality.
- Sound like you've been IN THE BOOTH covering this league all season. Reference past races naturally.
- Keep the scale right — this is a serious but small regional series with 9 drivers. Be enthusiastic and genuine, not over-the-top.
- Use car numbers ("the 88," "the 21 car") alongside driver names.
- One host can be more analytical (stats, power rankings), the other more narrative (interviews, drama). Let them play off each other.
- Friendly disagreements are great. "I think Nick's got this locked up." "I don't know, man — Nathan at his best is a different animal."
- QUOTE THE INTERVIEWS. React to what drivers actually said. This makes the podcast feel connected to the league.
- Reference the website's content: power rankings, news articles, Pick'em predictions, head-to-head stats. It makes the coverage feel deep and layered.
- Talk about incidents as crashes, bent fenders, wall contact — not numbers on a screen. "He tore up fenders all night" not "he had 42 incident points."
- Total episode should feel like 10-15 minutes of natural conversation.

2. Justin uploads CSV results to the league website
3. From the league website, gather:
   - **Race results** (finishing order, laps led, incidents, points)
   - **Updated standings** (with drops applied)
   - **Power rankings** (all-race data, no drops)
   - **Post-race interviews** (quote the best ones)
   - **Pre-race interviews** for next week (if already posted)
   - **News article** (post-race recap)
   - **Pick'em results** (who picked whom, accuracy)
   - **Head-to-head stats** (any notable matchups)
   - **Awards tracker** (milestones, leader changes)
4. Update the League Context Document if standings or storylines have changed significantly
5. Fill in this template with ALL of the above data
6. Paste the filled-in prompt into NotebookLM's Customize field
7. Generate the podcast
8. Upload the MP3 to the league website via Admin > Podcasts
9. Share with the league!
